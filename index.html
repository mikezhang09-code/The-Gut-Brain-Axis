The Receiving End
Gut signals arrive at the nucleus tractus solitarius (NTS) in the brainstem, which relays to the dorsal raphe nucleus (mood), locus coeruleus (alertness), and the amygdala, hippocampus, and prefrontal cortex — shaping emotions, memory, and decision-making.
Correlation observed in published studies — not proof of direct causation in humans.
The Vagus Highway
The longest cranial nerve, the vagus, carries signals at ~1–18 m/s depending on fiber type (C-fibers to Aδ). Neuropod cells in the duodenum form direct glutamatergic synapses with vagal neurons, enabling millisecond neurotransmission — far faster than hormonal signaling. EC cells release serotonin via TPH1, activating vagal 5-HT3 receptors.
Kaelberer et al., Science 2018; Int J Mol Sci 2025 — neuropod synaptic transduction and EC-vagal serotonin.
The Microbial Factory
Your colon hosts trillions of bacteria that ferment dietary fiber into short-chain fatty acids (SCFAs) — acetate, propionate, and butyrate. These metabolites upregulate TPH1 to boost serotonin production and enter the bloodstream, eventually crossing the blood-brain barrier via MCT transporters on brain endothelial cells.
Int J Mol Sci 2023 (PMC10606032); Frontiers in Endocrinology 2020 — SCFA transport and BBB crossing.

The Deep Dives

Each pathway tells a different story about how your body's inner world reaches your brain. Tap to explore the science behind each one.

01
The Neuropod Express
Neuropod cells form direct glutamatergic synapses with vagal neurons, transmitting nutrient signals to the brain in milliseconds. A second colonic neuropod circuit uses TLR5 to sense bacterial flagellin and release PYY to regulate feeding. EC cells amplify with serotonin via TPH1.
Kaelberer et al., Science 2018; Bohórquez, Kaelberer et al., Nature 2025
Explore mechanism
02
The Metabolite River
Gut bacteria ferment dietary fiber into SCFAs — acetate, propionate, and butyrate. These metabolites ride the bloodstream and cross the blood-brain barrier through MCT transporters on brain endothelial cells, influencing neuroinflammation and neurotransmitter levels.
J Neuroinflammation 2025; Frontiers in Endocrinology 2020
Explore mechanism
03
The Slow Burn
When gut barrier integrity weakens — from stress, poor diet, or dysbiosis — bacterial components like LPS translocate into the bloodstream, triggering cytokine cascades (TNF-α, IL-6) that reach the brain over hours to days, associated with changes in mood and cognition.
Cell Reports Medicine 2025; Nature 2024
